Your task:


In this part of the study, you will guess whether a company’s earnings are high or low after getting advice from another participant.
  • In total, there are 100 companies, 50 of which have high and 50 of which have low earnings. The computer secretly and randomly selects one of these companies, each with equal probability.
  • Your task is to guess what the selected company’s earnings are. Specifically, you have to indicate the the percentage chance that you think that the company’s earnings are high.
  • Before making your guess, you may receive a message from another participant. This other participant either sees the company’s actual earnings or does not get to see them. If they see them, they can choose between revealing this information to you and not revealing it. If they choose to reveal, you receive a message revealing the actual earnings. This means: the other participant cannot lie about earnings, should they choose to send a message. The other participant’s earnings depend on your guess: they make more money the higher your guess.
    • Whether the other participant observes the actual earnings is decided by a random computer draw. With some percentage chance, the other participant observes the earnings. With the remaining percentage chance, they observe nothing.
    • You will know this percentage chance, but you will not be informed whether the other participant was actually randomly selected to observe the company’s earnings.
    • If the other participant observes the earnings, they can choose to either Reveal or Not Reveal the actual earnings to you.
      • If the participant chooses Reveal, you will get a message that truthfully tells you what the company’s actual earnings are.
      • If the participant chooses to Not Reveal, you will not get any message.
    • This means: if you do not get a message, it is either because the other participant was not selected to observe the company’s earnings, or because they were selected to observe the earnings but decided to Not Reveal to you.
    • The other participant’s reward depends on your guess. Specifically, the other participant’s chances of winning $20 increases in your guess about how likely it is that the company’s earnings are high.
      Other’s chance of winning $20 (in %): 100-0.01 ✕ (100-Your guess) ✕ (100-Your guess)
    • This means: The higher your guess, the higher their chances of winning money.
    • When choosing to Reveal or Not Reveal, the other participant knows that you know about their percentage chance of observing the earnings.
  • Your task is to guess whether the selected company’s earnings are high or low after potentially receiving a message from the other participant.
  • Whenever you do receive a message from the other participant, you will know the company’s actual earnings with certainty, and hence it is straightforward to make your guess. We will not ask you for your guess in those rounds.
  • Instead, we will only ask for your guess in rounds in which you don’t receive a message from the other participant.
  • In each round, you will be told the other participant’s chance of observing the company’s earnings. Your will then make your guess.
  • You can also guess fractions, such as 6.78%.
  • In total, you will complete 13 rounds of this task. Across these rounds, the other’s chance of observing actual earnings will vary. If one of the rounds of this task is selected to determine your bonus, only your decision in this one round will determine your bonus.


Your bonus payment:


Your decisions in this part of the study may affect your bonus. If a round in this part is selected for payment, you may win a bonus of $10. Your chances of winning that bonus depend on whether a round with or without a message gets selected.
  • If round with message selected: This means the other participant was selected to observe the company’s earnings and decided to Reveal. In this scenario, you will win the $10 with certainty. Recall that we will simply skip these rounds when asking about your guesses.
  • If round without message selected: This means that either the other participant was not selected to observe earnings, or they were selected to observe earnings but chose to Not Reveal. These are the 13 rounds in which we ask for your guesses. You win the bonus of $10 if your guess is not more than 5 percentage points away from the statistically correct guess. All this means is that you should provide your best guess of how likely you think it is that earnings are high when you didn’t receive a message.
where Truth=100 if earnings are hight, and Truth=0 if earnings are low. While this formula might look complicated, all this means is that you should provide your best guess of how likely you think it is that earnings are high when you didn’t receive a message.

Your certainty:


In each round, we will ask you two questions:
  • You will make your guess that the firm’s earnings are high
  • We will ask you how certain you are about your guess. Specifically, we are interested in how likely you think it is (in percentage terms) that your guess is actually the statistically correct guess.
