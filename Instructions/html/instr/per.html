Your task:


In this study, you will will estimate the number of black dots shown to you on a screen.
  • In each of multiple rounds, you will see a box containing black dots for a second.
  • Each box contains somewhere between 50 and 150 dots.
  • After the box disappears, you have 10 seconds to type in your estimate of how many dots the box contained.
  • In total, you will complete 11 rounds of this task. Across these rounds, the number of dots in the box varies. These rounds are completely independent from one another.


Your bonus payment:


Your decisions in this part of the study may affect your bonus payment. If a decision in this part is selected for payment, you receive $10 if your estimate is within +/-1 of the correct answer. If you take longer than ten seconds to type in your answer, you will not get a bonus.


Example:



  • The dots would only be shown to you for a second.
  • After the dots disappear, you have 10 seconds to type into the box the number (between 50 and 150) of dots you think are in the box.


Your certainty:


In each round, we will ask you two questions:
  • How many dots you think are in the box.
  • We will ask you how certain you are about your decision. Specifically, we are interested in how likely you think it is (in percentage terms) that your estimate is actually correct.
